## Key Takeaways

1. **May and September were the months to be invested.** Combined +29.5% average.

2. **March was brutal.** -11.4% average. All tickers negative. Market-wide correction.

3. **HIMS is a wild ride.** Led 5 months but crashed 4 months. +67.7% in May, -34.4% in August.

4. **PLTR and GOOGL had the best combination of consistency and returns.** 81.8% win rate, strong YTD.

5. **Traditional seasonal patterns didn't hold.** May was strong (not weak). September was strong (not weak).

6. **Q2 and Q3 were nearly identical in strength.** +25% and +24.7% average.

7. **November pullback suggests profit-taking.** -5.4% average, 7/10 tickers negative.

8. **Indexes (SPY, QQQ) were most consistent.** 72.7% win rate, steady gains.

9. **AMZN was the worst performer.** Only +5.6% YTD despite being a mega-cap.

10. **Volatility pays if you can stomach it.** HIMS +47.5% despite only 54.5% win rate.

## Implications for Trading

### For Monthly Rotation Strategies:
- Buying in April and holding through May captured +21.6% average
- Buying in August and holding through September captured +14.8% average
- Avoiding March saved -11.4% average

### For Momentum Trading:
- HIMS led 5 months - momentum works on volatile stocks
- GOOGL led 3 consecutive months (Aug-Nov) - trends persist
- PLTR's April explosion (+41.2%) was followable

### For Risk Management:
- March correction was unavoidable (all tickers down)
